Kusatsu Onsen Day 2 - Otaki-no-Yu, Gunma Beef BBQ & Temple Visit Grand Finale

The final day of our Kusatsu Onsen trip, September 2nd, 2023. Starting with breakfast, experiencing the hot springs at Otaki-no-Yu, enjoying exquisite Gunma beef BBQ, and visiting Kosen-ji Temple - here’s our report of a fulfilling second day.

Breakfast at the Ryokan

Checking the hotel shuttle bus schedule to plan Day 2

Detailed map of Kusatsu Onsen - checking today’s destinations

First thing in the morning, we planned our second day using the hotel shuttle bus schedule and Kusatsu Onsen map. Planning for efficient travel time to Otaki-no-Yu and lunch locations is crucial for making the most of our time.

Authentic Hot Spring Experience at Otaki-no-Yu

“Otaki-no-Yu,” the representative day-trip hot spring facility of Kusatsu Onsen

The main event of Day 2 was “Otaki-no-Yu,” the most popular hot spring facility in Kusatsu Onsen. Famous for its “awase-yu” (gradual temperature adjustment) system, this facility allows you to enjoy Kusatsu’s hot springs gradually.

The spring quality is acidic, sulfur-containing aluminum sulfate and chloride hot spring with a pH of 2.1, characterized by strong acidity. We thoroughly enjoyed Kusatsu’s famous waters, which have high sterilization effects and are expected to benefit the skin.

Exquisite! Gunma Beef BBQ Lunch

After refreshing ourselves in the hot springs, we headed to the much-anticipated Gunma beef BBQ lunch.

Charcoal BBQ setup complete - a moment of rising anticipation

Premium charcoal grill ready for the finest Gunma beef

Beautiful marbled Gunma beef - a feast for the eyes

The moment premium beef meets charcoal fire

Perfectly grilled Gunma beef - the aroma is incredible

The ultimate moment - savoring the exquisite taste

Gunma beef is one of Japan’s premium wagyu brands, known for its exceptional marbling and tender texture. Grilled over charcoal, the meat’s natural flavors were maximized. The sweetness of the fat and the deep umami of the meat created an unforgettable culinary experience.

Town Exploration and Shopping

After lunch, we took time to explore Kusatsu’s hot spring town and do some shopping.

Traditional Japanese sweets shop in the hot spring town

Display of Kusatsu’s local specialty products

The hot spring town is lined with traditional sweet shops and souvenir stores. Local specialties include hot spring manju (steamed buns), yubatake cookies, and sulfur soap - all perfect mementos of our Kusatsu visit.

Spiritual Conclusion at Kosen-ji Temple

Approach to Kosen-ji Temple - a place of spiritual tranquility

The solemn main hall of Kosen-ji Temple

Peaceful temple grounds surrounded by nature

The historic temple bell

To conclude our Kusatsu Onsen journey, we visited Kosen-ji Temple. This historic temple, nestled in nature, provided a perfect spiritual ending to our hot spring trip. The sound of the temple bell echoing through the mountain air created a deeply moving moment.

Homeward Journey

Platform at the local station - time to head home

The local train that will take us on the first leg of our journey home

Beautiful scenery along the route home

Our time in Kusatsu Onsen came to an end, and we began our journey home. The local train ride through Gunma’s mountains provided beautiful scenery, allowing us to reflect on our wonderful two-day experience.
